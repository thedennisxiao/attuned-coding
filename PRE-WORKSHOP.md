## Questions to Sit With

These aren't homework. They're invitations.
You don't need to answer them in writing or arrive with conclusions.
Just let them move through you in the days before we meet.

**On your relationship to technology:**
- What is your current relationship to technology? To AI specifically?
- When you think of AI, what arises first — in your body, before your mind?
- Is there fear? Curiosity? Dismissal? Something else?
- Can you be with whatever that is, without needing to change it?

**On what matters to you:**
- What do you keep wishing existed in your life or work?
- What would make your life 1% more easeful or enjoyable, long-term?
- What's something you find beautiful — in nature, in design, in how people move?
- If you knew you couldn't fail and had help building anything —
  what would you make?

**On your lineage:**
- What practices, teachers, or traditions have most shaped how you show up in the world?
- What do you carry from that — in your body, in your values, in how you see?
- When you create something — a meal, a session, a letter — what are you putting into it?

## What to Expect

- This is not a bootcamp. You will not be overloaded.
- You will be asked to feel things as much as to learn things.
- We will move slowly and pause often.
- You don't need to know how to code. You need to be willing to be present.
- By the end, you'll have started something — even if it's small.
