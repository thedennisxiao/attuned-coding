# Attuned Coding
## Coding with More Than Just Your Mind

---

## The Essential Insight

There is a gap. On one side: attuned practitioners — people who have done
the inner work, who carry lineages of embodied presence, who know how to
be with what is. On the other: technology, accelerating, mostly unconscious,
shaped by people who see everything as a resource to be optimized.

The gap is not inevitable. It exists because too few attuned people have
crossed into creation.

We need more attuned people to create.
We need people creating to be more attuned.

## The Consciousness in What We Create

We bring all of ourselves to whatever we do in the world — most especially
what we create. It is not only *what* is built but *who* is building it
and *how* they are when they build it.

A Hakomi practitioner building a focus app imbues something fundamentally
different than a productivity bro building the same app. The consciousness,
the lineage, the quality of presence — all of it moves through.

This is why it matters who is creating. This is why now is the time.

## The Distinction That Matters

Heidegger describes two ways of relating to the world:

**Technological attunement**: everything is a resource, a means to an end.
This is the dominant narrative around AI — fear, displacement, disconnection.

**Poetic attunement**: seeing the truth, beauty, and goodness in what is.
Not naive. Not uncritical. Open to wonder.

We choose our attunement. What we create reflects it.

With AI coding tools, *how* you work matters more than *what* you know.
This makes now the right moment. You don't need to be technical.
You need to be present.

## The Audience

Attuned practitioners — Hakomi, Aletheia, Art of Accomplishment, coaching,
somatic work, contemplative traditions — and anyone who carries the quality
of deep presence and care for human flourishing, regardless of lineage.

Also: those who come wanting to learn to code, but who feel drawn to a
more attuned way of doing so. In the process, they may learn to attune
to themselves. That is possible too.

## The Transformation (Qualitative)

Participants leave not necessarily having "built something" (though that
often happens) but having shifted in how they relate to themselves as creators.

Stories dissolve: "I'm not technical." "AI disconnects us." "This isn't for me."
A felt sense of expansiveness opens. Freedom — not from technology,
but in relation to it.

The measure is not what they made. It's whether they leave feeling more possible.

## What Participants Leave With (Concrete)

- **A working relationship with Claude** — they know how to have a real
  conversation, not just issue commands
- **Their own CLAUDE.md** — written, reflective of their values and intentions
- **A mental model of AI** — the gasoline metaphor: demystified, not dwelt on
- **A curated skill set installed** — compound-engineering, impeccable design,
  frontend-design, and others selected for this cohort; participants can install
  and use them independently after the workshop
- **A project started** — something that actually matters to them, seeded
  from their own inquiry
- **Fluency in the language** — they know what a CLAUDE.md is, what a skill
  is, what Git is, what Vercel is; enough to orient themselves and keep going

## The Spirit of the Teaching

Hakomi-inflected. Gentle invitation, not push. When fear or aversion arises —
we don't work around it. We bring curiosity to it.

"What is your relationship to AI?" becomes a mindfulness practice, not a debate.
Non-violence toward existing stories. Loving presence while new possibility
finds its way in.

We pause every 20–25 minutes. We don't rush.

The documentation of this process — seed to sapling — is itself part of
the course. Students witness what can be built, and what the process looks like.

## The Secure Container

The workshop is held by the 5 qualities of secure attachment.
These are not ideals to aim for — they are the design of the space.

**1. Safety and protection**
We talk about using Claude Code safely and securely from the start.
A grounding meditation opens the day. The container is named explicitly.
People know what kind of space they're in before anything technical begins.

**2. Attunement / feeling understood**
We permission how people are actually feeling — not how they think they should feel.
Hakomi-style exercises in breakout rooms: one person speaks, the other reflects back:
"What I'm hearing you say is... Did I get you?"
No fixing, no reassuring, no pivoting to solutions. Just being received.

**3. Comfort, soothing, and care**
This comes from the pacing. A spacious workshop, not an information firehose.
We stay embodied throughout — movement, breath, Ground app as anchor.
Technical content is delivered from a grounded, unhurried place.

**4. Expressed delight**
We celebrate what people are building and discovering — visibly and genuinely.
A WhatsApp group (or similar) for sharing during and after.
A course website where participants' projects are shown and honored.
Their work matters. We say so.

**5. Support for exploration**
The container itself is the support — practitioners alongside practitioners,
moving into unfamiliar territory together. Nobody is alone in not knowing.
Office hours and a follow-up session extend the holding beyond the day itself.

## What This Is Not

- A technical bootcamp
- A productivity course
- An AI advocacy pitch
- A replacement for the lineages participants carry — it honors them
