# CLAUDE.md — Attuned Coding

## What This Project Is

A workshop (and eventually a course) for attuned practitioners and others
drawn to a more conscious way of creating with AI. Not technical training —
an invitation into a new way of being in relation to technology.

See SOUL.md for the full philosophy and OUTLINE.md for the curriculum.

## Tone and Voice

- Contemplative, warm, unhurried — not productivity-bro, not technical-bro
- Accessible: participants may not know what a terminal is
- Use plain language; explain concepts through metaphor before explanation
- The "gasoline" standard: if you wouldn't explain it that way to a
  thoughtful therapist who's never written code, simplify further
- Poetic when it serves clarity; never purple

## Audience

Practitioners (Hakomi, somatic, coaching, contemplative) and anyone with
the quality of deep presence — regardless of technical background.
Also: those drawn to code but seeking a more attuned path.

Assume: no prior technical experience. High capacity for felt sense,
nuance, and reflection.

## What "Done" Looks Like for Content

- A non-technical person reads it and understands what's being asked of them
- The philosophy shines through, not just the instructions
- It doesn't rush — it invites
- It honors what participants bring, not just what they're learning

## Decisions Already Made

- Title: Attuned Coding
- Subtitle: Coding with More Than Just Your Mind
- Workshop first, course later, in-person option always
- Rhythm: pause every 15–20 minutes; don't push
- Skills participants receive: compound-engineering, impeccable,
  frontend-design, every-style-editor (and others TBD)
- SOUL.md written before any workshop content; consulted throughout
- Document the build process live — seed to sapling is itself the content

## What to Avoid

- Technical jargon without immediate plain-language translation
- Rushing into "let's build" before the relational/philosophical ground is set
- Treating this like a coding bootcamp or productivity workshop
- Skipping the aversion/fear work — it's not optional, it's foundational
